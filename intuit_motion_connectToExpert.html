Context

After a customer selects a Callback or Chat in the help panel, TurboTax displays minimal information until it connects the customer to an expert. There was an opportunity for a small win to clarify and emphasize the process of connecting a customer to an expert by using a delightful animation.

Old experience

Customer Problem: "When I want to connect with an expert there is an allotted amount of time before I am connected. I don’t understand why I need to wait this long to get help, what is happening in the background, or even how much time is left before I connect. This makes me feel impatient and frustrated because I want help now."

Our hypothesis was that by being more transparent with the search process, we can increase the customer confidence and trust in the brand. We wanted our customers to have a moment of delight that makes them feel confident that they will be routed to an expert in no time and that Intuit has their back.

Competitor Benchmark

Connecting a user to a real time service is common in rideshare apps like Lyft and Uber. There was a trend in zooming out in the interface, then back in once the service was connected.

Lyft

Uber

It’s important to note that despite giving a concrete number for the countdown, it’s still a fluctuating estimate. Both services increased and decreased the countdown depending on driver availability without informing the user. Both services also had multiple states to slowly guide the user through the process.

Storyboards

There are several steps involved for connecting a customer to an expert. The customer first gets added to a queue after they submit the request. When an expert becomes available, they are assigned that customer. Analytics show that this step usually takes less than 5 minutes. The expert then has time to review the customer’s issue before connecting.

Storyboard

Goals:

  • Set user expectations with a countdown.
  • Increase user confidence by showing that Intuit has a large network of experts ready to serve.
  • Increase user clarity by showing that we are searching for the correct expert for them.
  • Increase user confidence by showing that the tax expert has prepared for their questions.

Explorations

I lost track of the earlier iterations but here are the final 2 that were shown to PM. These approaches centered around 3 animation states. State 1: Searching for an expert. State 2: Expert is found and is reviewing your questions. State 3: Connect to expert.

Mild: subtle pulses to show search

Wild: We're working hard to find the right expert for you

The team aligned on going with the mild approach with more emphasis on the pulsing red dots. They also wanted to remove magnifying glass in the second part of the animation to simplify it. The team felt that the wild design made it feel like our search technology was slow because it went from only one location to another. There were iterations of multiple lines firing at once but the animation became chaotic.
