Context

TurboTax Live lets customers connect to professional CPAs for tax advice. The live help experience was subpar, often with our experts barely in frame and not using the backdrop. There was an opportunity to educate the experts on optimal video presentation, thus improving the customer’s experience.

Marketing experience

Actual experience

Customer Problem: "When I connect with an expert, the experience is completely different from what was advertised. This makes me feel shocked and bamboozled, resulting in less trust for TurboTax."

The hypothesis was that by using Amazon’s Rekognition API, we can subtly train experts to maintain proper appearance protocol to ensure customers have a pleasant experience. Customers benefit by connecting to an expert who is in frame and has proper lighting. This gives them confidence that TurboTax experts are certified professionals who will help solve their problems.

Process

This project started when I came across Amazon’s Rekognition API. It can identify objects, people, text, scenes, and activities in images and videos, as well as detect any inappropriate content. I built a scrappy prototype demonstrating the possibilities of this tech.

Goals

Existing problems that this technology could solve:

  • Security: Some experts have been caught outsourcing their work.
  • Inappropriate content: Rekognition can detect unverified identities and inappropriate objects.
  • Position: Make sure experts are in frame.
  • Depth: Make sure experts aren’t too close or too far.
  • Lighting: Make sure experts have proper lighting.

At the time, Intuit shipped backdrops that were attached to our experts' chairs. There were several downsides to this physical approach:

  • Expensive: ~$1M/yr
  • Cumbersome: The backdrop took up 52" diameters and sits on the back of the expert's chairs
  • Logistically complicated: It had to be shipped separately from the laptop package
  • Difficult to ensure compliance: Many experts didn't even use the backdrop
  • Not effective: The backdrop didn't fully cover the environment

Backdrop

It was difficult to control the expert's physical environment

I came accross a web based background bluring API called TensorFlow's BodyPix API and baked it into the prototype to blur the webcam background. This feature was still buggy at the time but we wanted to spark a conversation around the benefits of investing into such technologies. Using this digital approach would save Intuit a tremendous amount of money every year and give the customer a more consistent experience.

Outcome

I showcased this to the product leadership team. There was full alignment behind the benefits of using Rekognition and BodyPix. It was added to the 2020 product road map and is now live in product.

Guiding expert to be in frame

There were still many design considerations that needed to be accounted for:

  • Would experts experience any negative effects now that they realize they’re being watched?
  • How do we subtly tell experts to reposition themselves without distracting them from helping the customer?
  • Would experts feel burned out from maintaining proper posture the entire time?
  • How rigorous should the AI be?

Ultimately, the goal of this side project was to convince product management to allocate resources to this initiative and that was achieved. I had to work on my main project, Pyro, so I passed all of these concerns to the product team that took ownership of this after me.
